Biography

1996: Origins

The first incarnation of what evolved into OneRepublic formed in 1996 after Ryan Tedder and Zach Filkins befriended each other during their senior year.

 

During a drive home, as Filkins and Tedder discussed favorite musicians including Fiona Apple, Peter Gabriel, and U2, they decided to assemble a band. They enlisted a few musical friends and named their rock act - "This Beautiful Mess".

 

After senior year ended, Tedder and Filkins parted ways, each attending different colleges.

2002-2007: Reformation

Reuniting in Los Angeles in 2002, Tedder and Filkins formed their second band under the moniker "Republic".

 

After a few lineup changes, the group finally settled with Tedder on vocals, Filkins on lead guitar and backing vocals, Eddie Fisher on drums, Brent Kutzle on bass and cello, and Drew Brown on guitar.

 

The band's name was changed to "OneRepublic" after the record company mentioned that the name Republic might result in legal action from other, similarly named bands.

 

The band worked in the studio for two and a half years and recorded its first full-length album and before it's release, it caught the attention of a number of labels, including Timbaland's Mosley Music Group.

 

The band soon signed onto the label, becoming the first rock band to do so.

2007–2011: Dreaming Out Loud and Waking Up

OneRepublic's debut album, Dreaming Out Loud, was released on November 20, 2007, and debuted at number 14 in the US with first week sales of 75,000. Critical reception to the album ranged from negative to mixed.

 

Their lead single, Apologize, was released in its original version from Dreaming Out Loud. Timbaland, who at the time was one of the most highly visible artists/producers, approached the band to be featured on a compilation album he was working on, Shock Value.

 

OneRepublic's second studio album, Waking Up, was released on November 17, 2009, charting at number 21 on the Billboard 200, and ultimately selling over 500,000 copies in the US and over 1 million worldwide.

 

The lead single, All the Right Moves, was released on September 9, 2009, peaking at number 18 on the US Billboard Hot 100 and being certified 2× Platinum.

 

In November 2011, OneRepublic released their first Christmas single, titled Christmas Without You.

 

Over the next years, the band supported many musicians, including Pink and Maroon 5.

2012–2015: Native and international success

OneRepublic released their third studio album, Native, on March 22, 2013, marking the end of the band's three-and-a-half-year hiatus. The album debuted at number 4 on the Billboard 200 becoming their first top 10 album in the US, with first week sales of 60,000.

 

Feel Again was released as the lead single on August 27, 2012, however after the album being delayed it was re-branded as a "promotional single". The song was released as a part of Save the Children's Every Beat Matters campaign, where a portion of the proceeds from the single's sales would be donated.

 

The album's third single, Counting Stars, has become their most successful single from the album thus far and the band's biggest hit. The song achieved significant international success and peaked at number 1 in 5 countries worldwide.

 

On October 29, 2015, the video Counting Stars became the first video by a band to reach 1 billion views on YouTube.

 

On April 2, 2013, the band embarked on their headlining concert tour "Native Tour" in promotion for the album, beginning in Europe. This ran throughout 2013/14 and saw the band perform shows across Europe, North America, Asia and Australia, and New Zealand.

2015–2017: Oh My My

In April 2015, Ryan Tedder stated in an interview that OneRepublic was going in a whole new direction and they are yet to recycle the same sound once on any song that they have ever put out.

 

In September 2015, it was confirmed that the band's fourth upcoming studio album would be released in early 2016.

 

On May 13, 2016, the first single Wherever I Go of the upcoming album was released.

 

OneRepublic posted on August 25, 2016, that the album would be called Oh My My, with a release date of October 7, 2016. The following week, the album's artwork was revealed.

 

Oh My My became the band's highest-charting debut to date, landing at No. 3 on the Billboard 200. On October 11, 2016, OneRepublic announced on their Twitter ("X") account that they would be releasing singles through 2018.

2017–2019: Standalone singles

Tedder published a Facebook post on April 27, 2017, revealing the band's future in releasing new material.

 

In the post, he stated that he dealt with "crippling anxiety" and became ill due to constant touring, recording, and promoting the band's releases. He clarified that OneRepublic would release "new music as often as humanly possible... monthly, weekly sometimes", departing from the typical album cycle system.

 

Singles they released in 2017 included No Vacancy, Truth to Power, Rich Love, and Born to Race.

 

Tedder stated at a concert that the band had started work on their fifth studio album that was due to be released sometime in 2018, which would include 7 to 8 songs.

2019–2021: Human

OneRepublic released the lead single Rescue Me on May 17, 2019, of their then-untitled fifth studio album. On August 27, 2019, OneRepublic announced their next single Wanted, which was released on September 6, 2019.

 

On September 15, 2019, Tedder announced that their fifth studio album would be called Human, in which he confirmed that Rescue Me and Wanted would be included on the album.

 

On March 10, 2020, One Republic announced over Instagram that Human was announced on the same day to be set for release on May 8, 2020.

 

As a result of the COVID-19 pandemic "requiring [the band] to distance [themselves] from each other and [fans]," in a statement to social media on April 8, 2020, the band announced the release of Human to have been postponed. It was "delayed indefinitely until fall." It was then delayed until 2021.

 

On July 1, 2021, the band announced that the album would be released on August 27, 2021. OneRepublic performed a livestreamed concert, One Night in Malibu, on October 27 and 28 through online platform Moment House in support of the release of Human.

2022–present: Upcoming sixth studio album

In February 2022, OneRepublic released the song West Coast. The song is the lead single from OneRepublic’s upcoming sixth studio album. The song is an ode to the American state of California and evokes classic 1960s California sounds, with inspiration from The Beach Boys, The Mamas and the Papas and Gorillaz .

 

They first started working on West Coast during the production process of their 2016 album, Oh My My, but ended up shelving it for later.

 

On May 13, 2022, the band released I Ain't Worried, the second single from the soundtrack for the film Top Gun: Maverick. It will also be featured on their upcoming sixth studio album which is rumored to be released by the end of the summer.

 

OneRepublic released the single Mirage featuring Mishaal Tamer for Assassin's Creed Mirage on September 22. A music video was released alongside the song.
